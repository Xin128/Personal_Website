Test Database

We mainly test our models on two tasks: the prediction of risk scores for the next visit and early readmission for patients with heart failure. We preprocess the Cerner data as a multi-level list including patient ID, the label for training, multiple visits, time differences and sequence of medical codes to be utilized for our models. Our visits have been time-stamped to build a temporal relationship between different visits for one patient. The sequence of our medical codes is mapped to ICD codes (the International Classification of Diseases), NDCs (National Drug Codes), LOINIC (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes).

Results

After testing and learning from these models, I successfully implemented RETAIN model for our cerner data, which was my first time building the model myself. Later I started to improve the speed of our model by splitting our data in our data loader instead of in the embedding of each minibatch, which significantly saves our training time and memory. In order to publish our final version, I also edited the Readme file for GitHub repo to make it clear and understandable.
